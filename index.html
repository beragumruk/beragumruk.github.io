Bera Gumruk

I’m a student at TJHSST working on computational economics, panel econometrics, market microstructure, and noisy operational data.

My economics research studies digital payments and informal activity using country panels, fixed-effects designs, clustered inference, wild-cluster bootstrap checks, alternative outcome measures, and leave-one-country-out robustness tests.

I’m also interested in ETF tracking error and perpetual futures market structure: funding rates, open interest, liquidations, oracle/reference prices, price deviations, liquidity fragmentation, and event-study designs around new perp markets.

A separate thread is industrial time-series work, especially defining reliability KPIs and anomaly-detection views from refinery sensor and control data. The common problem is separating signal from noise when the data is operational rather than clean.
