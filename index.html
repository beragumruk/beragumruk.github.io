Hi, I'm Bera. I'm a high school student at TJHSST interested in computational economics, especially how econometric models and market data can explain institutional behavior under uncertainty.

Right now, that means two main research threads: a country-panel study of digital payments and informality using fixed effects, clustered inference, wild-cluster bootstrap checks, and robustness tests; and market-microstructure work on ETF tracking error and crypto perpetuals, especially price/reference deviations, funding rates, open interest, liquidations, oracle pricing, bid-ask spreads, and liquidity fragmentation.

I also founded a quantitative finance club to teach risk modeling and market-data analysis to other students, and started a nonprofit that provides soccer equipment to kids worldwide. Long-term, I want to keep working near the boundary of quantitative research, economic institutions, and systems that affect real people.
